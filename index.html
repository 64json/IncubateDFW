Frequently Asked Questions

What is IncubateSF?

IncubateSF is a entrepreneural hackathon where high school and college students compete to create useful, real-world products with code.

What is a Hackathon?

A hackathon is an event, typically lasting several days, in which a large number of people meet to engage in collaborative computer programming.

When/Where?

This hackathon will take place at MakeSchool SF on August 6th.

Who can participate?

Any high school or college student is elligable to participate!

What should I bring?

Bring your laptop, toiletries, a sleeping bag, and any other technology you think you’ll need.

Do I need to know how to code?

No! We’ll have several workshops for people who want to learn how to code, and there are spots on teams for people who don’t code for people who are good at business or design.

Do I need to have a team?

No! We’ll have team forming sessions for those who don’t have one, and you can always hack solo if you want.

What is the process for me to apply?

Just register! More information will be sent out later!

How long can I hack for?

Hacking time lasts for 24 hours.

Is there a code of conduct?

Yes! Participants are expected to follow the MLH Code of Conduct.
tl;dr please respect all other participants and don't be a jerk.
We reserve the right to remove those who do not follow the code of conduct.
